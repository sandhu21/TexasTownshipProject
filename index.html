    About the Township

    A community worth
    exploring.

    Texas Corners is an unincorporated community nestled within Texas Township, Kalamazoo County — one of the fastest-growing and most livable communities in all of Southwest Michigan. First organized as a township in 1837, the area has gracefully evolved from an agricultural settlement into a destination defined by independently owned businesses, world-class natural spaces, and a genuine sense of neighborhood pride.

    The business district along Q Avenue serves as the social and commercial heartbeat of the community. Within just a few blocks you'll find craft breweries pouring award-winning ales, specialty food shops sourcing from local farms, grooming studios, home builders, golf simulators, and veterinary care — all operated by people who live here and care deeply about the place they serve.

    What makes Texas Corners truly rare is the combination of convenience and nature. Residents and visitors can walk from a coffee shop straight onto a trail system that winds through hundreds of acres of protected forest, wetland, and prairie. That balance — vibrant local commerce alongside unspoiled natural land — is simply hard to find anywhere else in Michigan.

    Life in Texas Corners

    A snapshot of the community that makes this place worth calling home.

    1837
    Year Established

    Texas Township was officially organized in 1837 as part of Kalamazoo County, making it one of the oldest townships in Southwest Michigan.

    36sq mi
    Township Area

    Texas Township covers approximately 36 square miles of Kalamazoo County, encompassing residential neighborhoods, nature preserves, farms, and the Texas Corners commercial district.

    17,691
    Residents

    The Charter Township of Texas is home to approximately 17,691 residents across ZIP code 49009, making it one of the more populous townships in Kalamazoo County.

    800+ ac
    Protected Land

    Al Sabo Land Preserve alone covers over 800 acres. Combined with Woollam Nature Preserve and the Township Trailway, thousands of acres of natural land are protected in perpetuity.

    12
    Dining Spots

    From Korean BBQ and craft breweries to classic corner bars and specialty coffee — Texas Corners punches far above its weight for dining variety in a community of its size.

    4
    Seasons to Explore

    Texas Corners comes alive in every season — summer splash pads and farmer's markets, fall color hikes through Al Sabo, winter grooming sessions, and spring trailway runs.
